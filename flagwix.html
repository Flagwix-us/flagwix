Popular Flag Materials and Their Advantages

Are you curious about the options for flag materials?

Let's take a look at three of the most common materials we use in flags. We will also discuss the benefits of each material so that you can make an informed choice when choosing your next flag.

Cotton

Cotton is an authentic flag material. It is great for indoor flying and for reenactors, but not for everyday flying.

Cotton is generally considered to be one of the least durable flag materials. If you plan to fly your flag every day and want it last years, then cotton might not be your best option.

Polyester

Heavy duty polyester is a great flag material choice if you love the look and feel cotton, but want to be able fly your flag every day. It is more durable than cotton and can withstand high winds better than cotton.

Polyester is a durable material that can be used to make larger flags for taller flagpoles.

Nylon

For most people, nylon flags are the best choice. These flags are strong and can fly in any breeze. They also dry quickly after rain.

Our Battle Tough nylon flags are a top seller. These flags are made with X-box stitching in the corners to increase durability. We also use a thicker thread throughout the flag for strength.
